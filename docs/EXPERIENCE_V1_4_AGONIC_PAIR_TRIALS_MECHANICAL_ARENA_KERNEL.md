## Core Law

The mechanical arena is a narrow pressure kernel:

```text
two agonic contestants
  + service witnesses
  + sealed stance commit
  + explicit charter
  + contradiction ledger
  + visible summon cost
  + lawful closure gate
  + bounded verdict request
  + scar and retention request boundary
```

Its laws are:

- No arena without charter.
- No contestant without agonic kind.
- No stance without sealed commit.
- No closure without trace.
- No closure over material open contradiction.
- No summon without visible request and cost.
- No scar without verdict and retention.
- No direct `Tree-of-Sophia` write.
- Assistants may serve the arena, not secretly become contestants.

## Blocking Contracts

The v1.4 center contract treats archive-local generated arena sessions, verdicts,
scar writes, retention schedules, storage records, dashboard outputs, and
`__pycache__` files as quarantine evidence unless an owner repository later
lands an adapted surface through its own gate. The center contract requires:

- landing states remain draft, proposed, reviewed, blocked, quarantined, or
  candidate-only; `active` is not a landing state
- live arena activation and live runtime activation are both forbidden
- only `agonic` forms may be primary contestants
- assistant forms may witness, notarize, carry sources, monitor, steward, or
  chronicle, but may not judge or become contestants
- two-contestant duel is the only v1.4 arena shape; third permanent contestant
  or swarm debate is out of scope
- sealed stance commit is required and post-hoc commit edits must block
- closure requires intact trace, no material open contradiction, valid bounded
  verdict, and no unresolved protocol breach
- hidden summon and costless summon both block
- Codex, assistants, routing, stats, KAG, memo, SDK, and runtime cannot issue
  arena verdicts
- scar and retention surfaces are request-only here; durable write and
  execution are forbidden
- runtime storage records, workers, host ports, schedulers, and daemons are
  forbidden
- direct `Tree-of-Sophia` runtime writes, node paths, and canon writes are
  forbidden
- generated clean-flow examples may inform checks but must not become owner
  truth
