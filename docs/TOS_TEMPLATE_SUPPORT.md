# ToS Template Support

This document records the current sixth-wave AoA doctrine for supporting ToS corpus scaffold work without absorbing ToS template meaning.

It follows [TOS_GROWTH_SUPPORT](TOS_GROWTH_SUPPORT.md).
That note explains how AoA may support ToS growth law without authoring philosophical stance.
This note explains the next boundary: how AoA may support stable IDs, node templates, and scaffold examples while leaving corpus-facing meaning in ToS.

## Core rule

AoA may support ToS corpus scaffold work.

AoA does not author ToS node identity or template meaning.

The center should make the route visible, keep the ownership boundary explicit, and avoid becoming a second template repository.

## Ownership chain

Keep the owning roles explicit:

- `Tree-of-Sophia` owns stable public node IDs
- `Tree-of-Sophia` owns source-node and concept-node templates
- `Tree-of-Sophia` owns corpus-facing scaffold example discipline
- `Agents-of-Abyss` owns ecosystem routing, support doctrine, and anti-collapse coordination

## What AoA support may do

AoA may:

- route readers and contributors toward the ToS-owned scaffold surfaces
- keep neighboring layers from quietly re-authoring template meaning
- help preserve reviewability and source-of-truth discipline around the scaffold wave

## What AoA support may not do

AoA should not:

- restate ToS template law as if the center owned it
- assign ToS node identities from the center
- absorb corpus-facing examples as a convenience mirror
- turn scaffold examples into generic operational metadata

## Review question

The central review question is:

Does AoA make ToS scaffold work easier to support without becoming a second author of ToS node meaning?
