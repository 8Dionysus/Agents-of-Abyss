## Core Law

The `v1.9` center contract is a bounded continuity-weave grammar over the
already-landed routing layer:

```text
task or session signal
  -> predecessor check
  -> continuity thread candidate
  -> purpose / evidence / freshness / budget gate
  -> bounded weave
  -> receipt-backed reentry candidate
  -> stale / decay / retire decision
  -> replay and boundary integrity check
  -> canonical anchor boundary review
  -> owner landing or recharter request
```

Its laws are:

- No continuity without purpose, evidence, freshness, and budget.
- No false continuity.
- No private memory sovereignty.
- No assistant private-memory expansion.
- No Codex continuity approval.
- No memory owner theft.
- No personal-context overreach.
- No symbolic anchor evidence replacement.
- No stale-context dominance.
- No continuity-as-consciousness claim.
- No direct `Tree-of-Sophia` write.
- No runtime continuity installation.
- No dashboard or packet sovereignty.

## Continuity Boundary

`continuity weave` here is a bounded, reviewable reentry contour. It may name
thread candidates, require evidence, preserve privacy-minimized carry, request
stale decay or retirement, and route a candidate toward owner-local review.
It may not author durable identity truth, private selfhood, canon, runtime
installation, or hidden persistence.

`symbolic anchors` here are only cue-backed compression surfaces. They may help
reentry when live cues and evidence remain present. They may not replace
evidence, override freshness, or justify personal or canonical overreach.

`continuity` here is not consciousness, not self-agency proof, and not a claim
that the workspace has become a sovereign actor.
