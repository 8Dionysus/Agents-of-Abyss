# Counterpart Bridge

This document records the current third-wave doctrine for AoA after the trunk-first structural wave and the method-centered second wave.

It does not replace the rootline or the method spine.
It adds the bridge law that governs how ToS concepts and AoA operational forms may reflect one another without collapsing into one object.

## Core doctrine

The current third-wave counterpart bridge is:

1. **KAG is a bounded substrate, not an empire**
2. **counterpart edges are derived bridges, not identity claims**

## Ownership rule

The ownership boundary for counterpart work is explicit:

- `Tree-of-Sophia` owns conceptual origin
- source AoA repositories own operational meaning
- `aoa-kag` owns derived counterpart projections
- `Agents-of-Abyss` owns bridge policy and anti-collapse doctrine

## Counterpart vocabulary

Use this small vocabulary for ecosystem-level counterpart policy:

- `analogy` = a structural resemblance that helps orientation without asserting sameness
- `support` = an operational surface that reinforces or preserves a ToS concept in practice
- `tension` = a productive mismatch where the operational surface helps reveal the limit of a concept bridge
- `calibration` = a bounded proof or review discipline that keeps an operational claim aligned without becoming the concept itself

## Bridge law

Counterpart mapping is allowed when it stays:

- derived
- source-linked
- optional
- suggestive
- reversible

Counterpart mapping is not allowed to become:

- proof that a concept and an operational surface are the same thing
- a shortcut that writes operational meaning back into ToS source nodes
- a quiet transfer of source sovereignty from ToS or AoA repos into `aoa-kag`

## Practical rule

A valid counterpart edge should answer:

- what ToS concept or architecture note is being bridged?
- what operational source surface is being referenced?
- what mode is being claimed: `analogy`, `support`, `tension`, or `calibration`?
- what evidence makes the bridge useful?
- what non-identity note keeps the bridge honest?

If those answers are not explicit yet, the bridge is still intuition, not public doctrine.
