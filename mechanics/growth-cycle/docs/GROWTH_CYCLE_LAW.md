# Growth Cycle Law

Growth Cycle is the reviewed movement law for agent-process growth.

It is not a scheduler, score, hidden automation loop, or universal owner. It is
a route that asks: what reviewed state are we in, what stage comes next, what
must remain provisional, and who owns the stronger truth?

## Stage Order

The default order is:

1. checkpoint intake
2. reviewed closeout chain
3. donor harvest
4. progression lift
5. route forks
6. automation opportunity
7. diagnosis gate
8. repair cycle
9. quest promotion
10. owner followthrough

Stages may be skipped only when the evidence says they are irrelevant. Stages
may repeat only when the repeat is explicit and reviewed.

## Review Law

Checkpoint evidence is provisional. Closeout evidence must be reviewed before
the center claims harvest, progression, repair, memory, quest, or owner
movement.

## Owner Law

The center may route a stage. It may not land the stronger object. Owner
repositories accept, implement, prove, remember, execute, publish, or reject the
object in their own domain.

## Stop-lines

- no hidden scheduler
- no autonomous self-repair claim
- no proof without eval-owned verdict
- no memory without memo-owned review
- no runtime activation without runtime-owner gates
- no quest promotion without questbook and owner evidence
- no owner acceptance without owner-local receipt
