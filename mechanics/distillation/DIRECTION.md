# Distillation Direction

Distillation turns heavy material into reviewable active form without letting
the center become the owner of every result.

## Direction

The mechanic should make conversion legible in this order:

1. name the source material and its review state
2. preserve the raw route without making it the active route
3. extract the surviving function into the smallest useful active surface
4. prune noise, duplicates, stale wording, and packet scaffolding
5. keep receipt or provenance indirection when older material must remain cited
6. route candidate truth to the stronger owner
7. validate that no authority was inflated during conversion

## Working standard

Active files in this package should be short route surfaces. Source lineage and
archival accounting live behind `PROVENANCE.md`. Owner-local asks live in
`OWNER_REQUESTS.md`.

## Not this mechanic

- It is not generic summarization.
- It is not proof.
- It is not memory canon.
- It is not owner acceptance.
- It is not runtime activation.
- It is not ToS compost or canon authority.
- It is not permission to delete raw history without a separate retention law.

## Current priority

Keep Distillation as the bridge between healthy accumulation and active
clarity: new packets may arrive, but active routes stay light, readable,
source-linked, and owner-routable.
