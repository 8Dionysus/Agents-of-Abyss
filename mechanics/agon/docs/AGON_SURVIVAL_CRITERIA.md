# Agon Survival Criteria

This document defines the criteria by which existing AoA surfaces are judged after the Agon turn.

A surface does not survive because it already exists. It survives because it can serve the future arena without becoming a quiet empire, a false witness, or a decorative myth.

## Survival axes

### 1. Lawful conflict support

The surface must help future conflict become reviewable state change rather than loose argument, prompt theater, or social fog.

A surviving surface should strengthen chartering, admissible moves, boundaries, escalation, evidence posture, witnessability, or reviewable outcomes.

### 2. Source ownership discipline

The surface must preserve owner-owned meaning.

If a center surface repeats layer-owned detail, it must route back to the owner. If a derived surface looks stronger than its source, it must be weakened or rechartered.

### 3. Actor formation readiness

The surface must not assume that current role profiles already implement full subjecthood.

Agonic actors and civil/service assistants must become distinguishable before the arena opens.

### 4. Witnessability

The surface must make claims inspectable through named references, schemas, examples, validators, receipts, or review paths.

Unwitnessed authority is not Agon-ready.

### 5. Memory and scar separation

The surface must not store durable scars, battle history, retention checks, or memory truth inside role profiles, routing hints, stats dashboards, or center doctrine.

Scars belong to memory surfaces when those surfaces exist.

### 6. Proof separation

The surface must not turn proof into governance.

Evals may judge bounded claims. They must not become the sovereign owner of routing, readiness, actor identity, or canonization.

### 7. Derived observability restraint

Stats may summarize movement. Stats must not become a scoreboard court, a ranking empire, or the source of victory.

### 8. Routing restraint

Routing may detect gates and next hops. It must not narrate truth, decide meaning, or replace owner receipts.

### 9. Assistant anti-drift

Civil/service assistants must remain predictable, externally revised, transparent, and non-contestant unless explicitly rechartered.

Hidden warrior drift is a protocol poison.

### 10. ToS canon restraint

Arena outcomes may eventually produce candidates. They must not write directly into `Tree-of-Sophia` or treat arena victory as canon.

The path remains slow and reviewable.

### 11. Runtime restraint

Runtime services, durable storage, transport, and control-plane helpers must not be claimed before their owners implement them.

A doctrine is not a service.

### 12. Reversibility

Every new Agon-preparatory surface should be reviewable, reversible, and small enough to correct.

Irreversible authority introduced before protocol law is a breach.

## Verdict scale

Use this scale for audits.

`survive`
: Keep the surface. It already supports Agon without authority drift.

`recharter`
: Keep the useful material, but rewrite its authority, scope, or ownership boundary.

`defer`
: Do not land it yet. The right owner or prerequisite wave is missing.

`prune`
: Remove or collapse it because it duplicates, decorates, or misleads.

`quarantine`
: Keep visible only with explicit warnings because consumers may mistake it for live truth.

## Death marks

A surface should not pass if it does any of the following:

- treats current validation as proof of Agon-readiness;
- hides ownership changes inside language changes;
- claims live runtime without runtime contracts;
- turns a route into an authority;
- turns an eval into a government;
- turns stats into victory;
- turns memory into certification;
- turns a service assistant into an undeclared contestant;
- turns an arena win into ToS canon;
- adds ornamental combat language without state-change discipline.

## Minimum readiness for later waves

Wave I may begin only after Wave 0 can point to a center-owned imposition surface and a generated readiness capsule.

Wave III may not begin until actor formation and assistant civil rechartering have their own landed readiness surfaces.

The arena cannot read actors that have not yet been formed.
