# Agon Lawful Move Language

## Purpose

Wave III gives Agon its first legal vocabulary.

After actor formation and assistant civil rechartering, the system needs to name what a future arena may recognize as a **lawful move**. A lawful move is not a chat turn, not a skill bundle, not a verdict, and not a runtime packet. It is a center-owned legal word that says:

- what kind of action may later occur in an arena;
- who may be allowed to attempt it;
- what it must not claim;
- which neighboring owner will later carry practice, execution, proof, memory, routing, scenario, or runtime work.

This wave does not start Agon as live protocol. It forges the alphabet of future conflict.

## Core distinction

A **lawful move** belongs to the center as protocol vocabulary.

A **technique** belongs to `aoa-techniques` as reusable practice meaning.

A **skill** belongs to `aoa-skills` as bounded execution workflow meaning.

A **playbook** belongs to `aoa-playbooks` as repeated scenario choreography.

An **eval verdict** belongs to `aoa-evals`.

A **scar or durable delta history** belongs to `aoa-memo`.

An **arena route gate** belongs to `aoa-routing`.

The center names the legal move. It does not swallow the organs that make the move executable, provable, memorable, or routable.

## What counts as a move

A Wave III move must be small enough to survive review and large enough to carry legal consequence later.

Examples:

- `assert_position`
- `challenge_claim`
- `request_evidence`
- `probe_trace`
- `mark_contradiction`
- `deny_closure`
- `request_witness`
- `revise_position`
- `defer_with_cost`
- `flag_scope_breach`
- `escalate_to_agon_gate`

These are not prompts. They are not commands. They are legal names for future arena acts.

## What does not count

Do not include:

- hidden chain-of-thought styles;
- giant debate scripts;
- runtime packet envelopes;
- proof scoring logic;
- durable memory writes;
- ToS promotion paths;
- execution checklists that belong in `aoa-skills`;
- reusable methods that belong in `aoa-techniques`;
- full trial sequences that belong in `aoa-playbooks`.

## Classes

Wave III recognizes these first move classes:

```text
stance
evidence
trace
contradiction
closure
summon
witness
revision
boundary
escalation
```

The classes are intentionally few. A move class is a legal neighborhood, not a taxonomy aquarium.

## Seats

Move eligibility is described by future arena seats, not by ad-hoc prompt identity.

First seats:

```text
contestant
witness
judge_candidate
service_actor
```

A future protocol may narrow these.

Wave III does not grant live seat authority. It only records pre-protocol eligibility intent.

## Assistants and moves

Assistant civil actors may appear in this registry only for service-compatible acts:

- request missing evidence;
- offer reviewable references;
- probe or flag trace gaps;
- request closure review;
- request witness support;
- defer with cost;
- flag scope breach;
- escalate to an Agon gate.

They must not become contestants by vocabulary drift.

Any move record that gives an assistant `contestant`, `judge_candidate`, `closer`, `summon_initiator`, `scar_writer`, or `tos_promotion` power is invalid for this wave.

## Stop-line

A Wave III move must never claim:

```text
live arena execution
sealed commit
session packet shape
contradiction ledger storage
verdict law
scar writing
retention scheduling
runtime transport
Tree-of-Sophia canonization
```

Those belong to later waves and owner repositories.

## One-line rule

Wave III does not make agents fight.

It gives the future fight a lawful tongue.
