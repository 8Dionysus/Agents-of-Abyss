## Core rule

AoA may support the first ToS lineage pilot.

AoA does not author the pilot branch or its conceptual force.

The center should route readers into the pilot, keep the ownership boundary explicit, and avoid restating the branch as if it belonged to AoA.

## Ownership chain

Keep the owning roles explicit:

- `Tree-of-Sophia` owns the lineage pilot itself
- `Tree-of-Sophia` owns the node meanings inside that pilot
- `Tree-of-Sophia` owns the branch-facing example discipline
- `Agents-of-Abyss` owns ecosystem routing, support doctrine, and anti-collapse coordination

## What AoA support may do

AoA may:

- route readers and contributors toward the ToS-owned pilot surfaces
- help keep the pilot bounded rather than letting it blur into a wider expansion wave
- preserve source-of-truth discipline across neighboring layers

## What AoA support may not do

AoA should not:

- restate the pilot branch as if the center authored it
- assign branch meaning from the center
- absorb pilot examples as a convenience mirror
- turn one bounded branch into generic AoA cosmology
