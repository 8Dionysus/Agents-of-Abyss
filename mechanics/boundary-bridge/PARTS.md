# Boundary Bridge Parts

Active parts are the default operating surface for boundary-bridge work. Use
the part that matches the crossing, then route proof, adoption, projection, or
implementation to the owner that must carry it.

## Part map

- [Boundary Contract](parts/boundary-contract/README.md): name the two sides,
  owner roles, bridge mode, and non-transfer rule before crossing.
- [Non-Identity Guard](parts/non-identity-guard/README.md): keep analogy,
  support, compatibility, and projection from becoming sameness claims.
- [Counterpart Edge](parts/counterpart-edge/README.md): use
  `analogy`, `support`, `tension`, and `calibration` without claiming identity.
- [ToS Support](parts/tos-support/README.md): support Tree-of-Sophia without
  authoring ToS meaning, source interpretation, or canon.
- [Witness Compost](parts/witness-compost/README.md): route witness material
  toward compost without turning memory, proof, runtime, or ToS canon into one
  object.
- [Derived Projection](parts/derived-projection/README.md): let KAG/export or
  graph-ready surfaces derive from sources without becoming source authority.
- [Owner Handoff](parts/owner-handoff/README.md): prepare owner-local landing
  packets without claiming owner acceptance.
- [Proof Review Route](parts/proof-review-route/README.md): distinguish owner
  receipt, source authority, eval proof, and public support claim.
- [Compatibility Route](parts/compatibility-route/README.md): keep SDK,
  routing, public projection, and adapters from becoming meaning owners.

## Provenance boundary

Do not open wave-era ToS support documents by default. Use
[PROVENANCE](PROVENANCE.md) when a bridge needs source trace, old route
history, or ToS-specific source doctrine.

## Active part contract

Every part keeps three working surfaces:

- `README.md`: when to use the part and where to start.
- `CONTRACT.md`: center boundary, allowed outputs, and stop-lines.
- `VALIDATION.md`: validation route, with executable commands centralized in
  `parts/AGENTS.md`.

A part may grow, split, merge, shrink, or retire when that improves its
function and keeps the route cleaner. The move should leave the active path
easier to follow, not merely smaller.
