# Constitution Runtime Law

The runtime implements already-authorized governance. It does not invent governance.

Article 1. Runtime action must reference a case, authority resolution, or sealed decision.
Article 2. Runtime enforcement must be scoped, expiring, and replayable.
Article 3. Codex may assist the runtime but may not hold sovereign authority.
Article 4. A council decision is invalid if vote sealing, quorum, or conflict-of-interest requirements fail.
Article 5. A precedent may be indexed only from a sealed, replayable decision.
Article 6. Direct Tree-of-Sophia runtime writes are blocked.
