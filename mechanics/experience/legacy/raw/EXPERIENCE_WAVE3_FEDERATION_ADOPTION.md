# Experience Wave 3: Federation Harvest And Adoption Forge

Experience Wave 3 plants v0.6 federation harvest and v0.7 adoption forge as
one bounded ecology.

Wave 2 asked whether a certified release could survive deployment pressure.
Wave 3 asks whether repeated post-release experience can become a shared AoA
pattern, and whether that pattern can return to owner repositories without
stealing authority.

## Source Seeds

- `aoa-experience-federation-harvest-seed-v0_6.zip`
- `aoa-experience-adoption-forge-seed-v0_7.zip`

## Federation Flow

```text
owner-local signals
  -> cross-repo recurrence
  -> federation pattern candidate
  -> harvest gate
  -> shared pattern registry
  -> KAG promotion dossier
  -> ToS candidate dossier boundary
  -> owner-local adoption quests
```

Federation harvest is active as a center-owned program route. It may collect
cross-repo signals, name recurrence, and route candidates to owner-local review.
It may not approve itself, write directly into `Tree-of-Sophia`, or promote a
derived pattern into KAG without evidence, retention, owner consent, and bounded
eval verdicts.

## Adoption Flow

```text
shared pattern registry
  -> adoption request
  -> owner consent
  -> compatibility matrix
  -> projection resolution
  -> shadow adoption run
  -> adoption decision
  -> owner-local patch
  -> assistant release | agonic trial | routing rule | playbook | skill | technique | runtime activation
  -> rollback or quarantine
  -> retention check
```

Adoption is owner-local. Harvest approval is not adoption authority.
Assistants adopt through versioned release, never hidden self-rewrite. Agonic
actors adopt through trial, scar, and retention. Routing may dispatch adoption
signals but may not author meaning. Stats may summarize adoption health but may
not judge. Memo may retain adoption lessons but may not prove claims. KAG may
downlink derived patterns but may not force owner uptake.

## Stop Lines

- no direct `Tree-of-Sophia` write from arena, deployment, Codex, assistant, KAG, or harvest surfaces
- no Codex approval of federation harvest
- no KAG promotion without evidence, retention, owner consent, and bounded eval verdicts
- no assistant hidden self-rewrite
- no agonic adoption without trial, scar, and retention
- no routing meaning theft
- no stats proof authority
- no memo proof authority
- no runtime activation without owner-local approval and rollback or quarantine fallback

## Owner Split

- `Agents-of-Abyss` owns the wave law, federation-level stop-lines, and center
  route.
- `Tree-of-Sophia` owns candidate intake boundaries and may reject direct
  runtime adoption.
- `aoa-evals` owns bounded harvest, lineage, owner-consent, ToS-boundary, and
  adoption verdict surfaces.
- `aoa-playbooks` owns recurring harvest, dossier, shadow adoption, rollback,
  and retention choreography.
- `aoa-stats` owns derived harvest/adoption summaries only.
- `aoa-memo` owns retention memories and adoption lessons only.
- `aoa-routing` owns navigation signals only.
- `aoa-agents` owns role and adoption posture contracts only.
- `aoa-sdk` owns typed helper contracts only.
- `aoa-kag` owns derived pattern candidate and downlink surfaces only.
- `aoa-skills` and `aoa-techniques` own owner-reviewed adoption receipts for
  their own objects only.
- `abyss-stack` owns storage, worker, event, and runtime record contracts only;
  seed landing is not live runtime activation.

## Exit Criteria

Wave 3 is valid when:

- the center example preserves v0.6 before v0.7;
- federation harvest cannot be approved by Codex;
- direct ToS write remains blocked;
- KAG promotion requires retention, owner consent, and bounded eval verdicts;
- adoption requires owner consent, compatibility, shadow evidence, decision,
  rollback or quarantine fallback, and retention;
- every owner-local schema/example pair validates in its owning repository.
