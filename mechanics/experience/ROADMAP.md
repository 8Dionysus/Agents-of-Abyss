# Experience Roadmap

Experience is planted as center doctrine. The current role is to keep active
parts discoverable, bounded, and receipt-backed while future owner-local
repositories decide what they can honestly activate.

## Current Contour

- Keep capture, certification, adoption, governance, release, office, service,
  continuity, runtime-boundary, and compatibility contracts routed through
  active `parts/`.
- Keep `LANDING_LOG.md` as the canonical Experience landing ledger.
- Keep `artifact-map.json` as the machine-readable receipt from old flat
  artifacts into active part homes.
- Keep `provenance-receipts.json` as the active receipt registry for old
  packets, staged seed inputs, and sibling-owner surfaces used by artifacts.
- Keep `PROVENANCE.md` as the only active bridge back to historical accounting.
- Keep validators, tests, schemas, and examples aligned with the active part
  that uses them.
- Keep owner-local adoption links absent until sibling repositories land real
  proof or owner acceptance.

## Next Work

- Add owner-local adoption links only when sibling repositories land real proof
  or an accepted owner-local receipt.
- Tighten part contracts where owner-request stop-lines become more specific.
- Keep incoming waves flowing through distillation instead of rebuilding flat docs.
- Update this roadmap when a mechanic change alters future work, route shape, or owner pressure.

## When Time Comes

Use this block for likely future moves that are not ready now. Each item should
name the condition that means the time has come, the move to make, and the
guardrail that keeps the active route clean.

### Landing-log index

Time has come when: `LANDING_LOG.md` grows too long for reliable manual
scanning, or the top route index starts lagging behind checked landing entries.

Move: add a validated or generated landing-log index and keep it under the
landing-log or generated-freshness checks.

Guardrail: do not move canonical landing receipts into archival provenance;
archive accounting preserves source provenance, not the active landing ledger.

## Out Of Scope

- Live workspace runtime activation.
- Owner-local office installation.
- Hidden memory, KAG, ToS, or assistant authority.
