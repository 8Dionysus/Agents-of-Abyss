# RPG Direction

RPG is the world-grammar mechanic for AoA.
It should make agent work more legible, consequential, and inhabitable without becoming a toy layer, a hidden ontology, or a runtime authority.

## Source-of-truth Split

- `README.md`: package entry card and shortest route.
- `DIRECTION.md`: current operating direction for RPG as world grammar.
- `USAGE.md`: active decision surface for when to use RPG language and when to stay plain.
- `ROADMAP.md`: future contour, not a historical ledger.
- `LANDING_LOG.md`: canonical landing ledger.
- `PARTS.md`: active part map.
- `parts/`: working RPG sub-mechanics.
- `parts/vocabulary-overlay/`: checked terminology, schema, example, generated mirror, and validator.
- `OWNER_REQUESTS.md`: center-side stronger-owner request packets.
- `PROVENANCE.md`: bridge to legacy evidence when active parts need historical support.
- `legacy/`: preserved historical sources and distillation receipts.
- `docs/`: compatibility route only.

## Current Direction

RPG should deepen from a thin presentation overlay into a disciplined language of world, role, quest, consequence, and progression.
That deepening is allowed only when the game form improves action quality.

Use RPG language when it clarifies at least one of these:

- who acts, from which role or origin, and under which owner boundary
- what the quest or campaign actually asks for
- what the stakes, stop-lines, and proof routes are
- what changed because of the action
- which reputation, unlock, caution, memory, or owner route should follow

Do not use RPG language when it only renames ordinary repo work with decorative labels.
The test is simple: if the RPG form does not improve routing, judgment, memory, proof, or consequence, leave it out.

## Why Language Matters

AoA is built for language-shaped agents.
A good RPG grammar can help a language model hold context as a world instead of a flat task list.

The useful power is not cosmetic fantasy.
The useful power is structured meaning:

- origins and classes make role limits visible
- parties make collaboration and handoff visible
- quests make obligations durable and reviewable
- campaigns make long arcs readable
- reputation makes quality history matter
- unlocks make permission evidence-backed
- resources make cost and restraint visible
- consequences make actions rememberable

## Functional Forms

RPG may grow through forms that already match AoA mechanics:

- `origin`: where an agent or route begins, including constraints and inherited posture
- `class`: readable role profile, grounded in `aoa-agents`
- `quest`: durable obligation, grounded in Questbook and owner repositories
- `campaign`: long-horizon route, grounded in `aoa-playbooks`
- `party`: bounded multi-agent or multi-owner collaboration
- `ability`: executable workflow reflection, grounded in `aoa-skills`
- `feat`: reusable practice reflection, grounded in `aoa-techniques`
- `reputation`: scoped trust reflection, grounded in proof and runtime owners
- `resource`: bounded cost or capacity reflection, grounded in runtime owners
- `chronicle`: memory and consequence reflection, grounded in `aoa-memo`

These forms are not authority by themselves.
They become useful only when tied to source refs, proof refs, owner routes, or runtime contracts.

## Integration Posture

- Questbook owns the durable quest object route; RPG may make quests playable and consequential.
- Agon owns lawful pressure; RPG may make pressure feel like trial, duel, rank, or scar only when proof and stop-lines survive.
- Experience owns lived-work contracts; RPG may turn experience into growth, reputation, office, or campaign language only after the owner route is clear.
- Recurrence owns return law; RPG may make return, re-entry, recovery, and continuity readable.
- `abyss-stack` owns runtime state; RPG may define projection needs but does not activate them.

## Stop-Lines

- Do not turn every task into a quest.
- Do not turn every label into a game stat.
- Do not create a universal power score.
- Do not let RPG vocabulary replace owner-local truth.
- Do not let presentation labels replace canonical machine keys.
- Do not claim runtime state, role canon, proof verdicts, or quest closure from RPG center docs.
- Do not make the project feel playful by making it less exact.

## Time Has Come When

- A repeated RPG form appears across two mechanics and needs a stable active contract.
- Agents need a world-shaped route to choose better than a flat TODO or plan.
- Questbook, Agon, Experience, or Recurrence produce a pattern whose RPG reading improves owner routing or consequence tracking.
- Runtime owners are ready to accept a projection contract without collapsing source meaning into UI state.

When that happens, grow RPG deliberately: name the form, bind the owner, add proof or stop-lines, and only then decide whether it belongs in `parts/`, `legacy/`, or an owner repository.

## Final Rule

RPG should make the project feel like a world because the work has consequence.
It must never make the project feel like a game because the language became decorative.
