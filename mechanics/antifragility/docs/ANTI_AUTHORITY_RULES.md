# ANTI_AUTHORITY_RULES

These rules prevent convenience surfaces from turning into quiet empires.

## Rule 1. The center routes, it does not replace
`Agents-of-Abyss` may define the federation map, layer roles, and
constitutional prohibitions.
It must not become the owner of repo-local commands, proofs, runtime behavior,
or memory truth.

## Rule 2. The product edge does not expand public truth casually
A product repo may present its current public surface.
It should not repeat ecosystem doctrine or accumulate status text that already
has a better canonical home.

## Rule 3. Techniques and skills do not impersonate each other
`aoa-techniques` owns reusable practice.
`aoa-skills` owns bounded execution workflows.
If the distinction disappears, the duplicate surface should disappear too.

## Rule 4. Evals prove, they do not govern
`aoa-evals` may emit bounded verdicts and blind spots.
It must not become the system's final authority on readiness, routing, or
ownership.

## Rule 5. Stats derive, they do not reign
`aoa-stats` may summarize source-owned receipts and bounded eval outputs.
It must not become a sovereign score surface or dashboard court.

## Rule 6. Routing navigates, it does not narrate truth
`aoa-routing` may suggest next hops and postures.
It must not explain what happened in place of owner receipts or proof.

## Rule 7. Memo recalls, it does not certify
`aoa-memo` may preserve reviewed context.
It must not certify present health, current readiness, or factual correctness
by itself.

## Rule 8. Agents and playbooks narrow action, they do not widen ownership
`aoa-agents` and `aoa-playbooks` may shape handoffs and recurring scenarios.
They must not redefine repo ownership or smuggle in hidden autonomous mutation.

## Rule 9. KAG derives, it does not outrank sources
`aoa-kag` may organize retrieval-ready projections.
It must not become a hand-authored substitute for source repos or a shadow
canon.

## Rule 10. SDK and runtime helpers stay explicit
`aoa-sdk` and `abyss-stack` may offer typed seams and runtime substrate
support.
They must not hide policy, broaden mutation, or auto-repair without explicit
reviewable posture.

## Correction rule

When a surface breaks one of the rules above, the first corrective move is to
shrink its authority, not to write another compensating doc around it.
