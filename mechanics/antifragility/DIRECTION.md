# Antifragility Direction

Antifragility keeps AoA able to grow after stress without becoming heavier,
more centralized, or more theatrical.

The current direction is practical:

- stress should leave clearer owner boundaries
- subtraction should preserve provenance
- fragile patterns should become reviewable routes
- repair claims should route to proof before they harden
- memory should retain lessons without becoming authority
- derived summaries should stay weaker than source receipts

## Current contour

Antifragility now works as an active mechanic with parts. The root docs explain
doctrine; the parts explain how to apply it in work.

Use this mechanic when a proposed change adds surface area, reacts to failure,
narrows authority, removes stale material, or decides whether a cleanup belongs
in the center or in an owner repository.

## Growth rule

Growth is welcome when it increases legibility, owner truth, proof posture, or
safe re-entry. Growth is suspect when it only adds another surface around a
boundary that should have been narrowed.

When a thing is not done now but clearly belongs later, record the condition in
`ROADMAP.md` rather than hiding future work inside active parts.

## Stop-lines

- No one-score health.
- No deletion theater.
- No center-owned cleanup execution.
- No hidden authority through helper surfaces.
- No historical source material in default active routes except through
  `PROVENANCE.md`.

## Next owner route

Owner-local repair routes to the owner repository. Proof routes to `aoa-evals`.
Retained lessons route to `aoa-memo`. Recurring cleanup choreography routes to
`aoa-playbooks`. Derived visibility routes to `aoa-stats`.
