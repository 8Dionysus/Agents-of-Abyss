# AoA Charter

## Purpose

Agents of Abyss (AoA) is a modular operational federation for building, evolving, and governing long-horizon agentic systems.

`Agents-of-Abyss` is the constitutional polis of that federation. It keeps the overall shape of AoA clear while implementation, proof, memory, runtime, authored knowledge, and recurring practice live in their owning repositories.

The center is allowed to name the city. It is not allowed to swallow every district.

## Constitutional authority

This charter is the center's authority boundary. It answers whether a claim may
be made by `Agents-of-Abyss` at all.

Operational routes, local editing rules, and validation commands live in
`AGENTS.md`, local `AGENTS.md` cards, route contracts, validators, and package
surfaces. This charter gives those routes their center boundary; it does not
replace them.

## Mission

AoA exists to support sustained intellectual and technical work where:

- growth is routed to the owner that can carry it
- agency remains bounded, reviewable, evidence-linked, and reversible
- source, derived, routing, runtime, memory, proof, and authored meaning stay legible as separate kinds of truth
- public claims can be checked against human docs, generated capsules, validators, and owner evidence
- reusable practice, executable workflows, proof, recall, role contracts, scenarios, substrates, and runtime bodies can mature without collapsing into one repository

## Center discipline

A center claim is healthy when it makes the owner split easier to inspect,
improves reviewability, and gives the next route a clearer owner.

When a center document begins to define repo-local behavior, it should shrink
back to routing, stop-lines, or an explicit owner request.

A root file is healthy when it serves the public civic surface. Historical
receipts, audit artifacts, generated objects, and future design notes belong in
their proper districts rather than beside the constitution.
